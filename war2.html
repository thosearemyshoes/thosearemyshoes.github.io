World War 2

The United States entered World War 2 after the Japanese bombed Pearl Harbor. However, initally the United States tried to remain neutral when Nazi Germany attacked Poland and initiated the beginning of World War 2. World War 2 began September 1st, 1949. As terrible as war is, it was the active war economy that solidified the United States rise out of the Great Depression.

On December 7th, 1941 the Japanese launched a devastating attack on the Pearl Harbor Military Base in the Hawaian Islands. This attack was a complete ambush and 2,500 military men lost their lives. It was after the attack on Pearl Harbor that FDR declared war with these famous words:



Because of the Japanese Attack on Pearl Harbor there was a lot of distrust of anyone of Japanese descent including Japanese-Americans. Japanese-Americans, many of whom had never even been to Japan, were forced into internment camps. Life was harsh and unfair for these citizens of the United States. This was a dark day in America's history.


The Manhattan Project began as the Allied Forces realized World War 2 was escalating. Scientists from the United States, Canada, and England worked together to create the first nuclear generator. This was the Manhattan Project.
On August 6th, 1945 the United States dropped an Atomic Bomb on Hiroshima, Japan. Three days later, the same fate was delivered to Nagasaki, Japan. The result of these bombs were devastating. Hundreds of thousands of people were killed instantly, and those that lived had permanent injuries and radation sickness. These bombs ushered in the end of World War 2.





From the prosperity of the Roaring Twenties, to the poverty of the Great Depression, and the patrioitism stemming from World War 2, the United States of America went through many social, political, and economic changes. American Citizens showed their strength and their resolve through all of these changes.
